Introduction to Redstone Engineering.

Redstone has many uses. The basics of redstone are simple. Wires that connect to lights, doors, pistons, trapdoors, or perhaps they connect to a repeater, which will extend its connection reach. Redstone can be found underground. As of right now, the version is 1.16. The 1.17 version of Minecraft will come out soon, so it will be different from now, but it will stay the same in the way that redstone will be found deep underground.

Redstone Door

A redstone door can be used to hide your base from greedy thieves in a multiplayer world. They often will run around looking for bases, and quietly take a few items from you, like iron, wood, stone, leather, and if you’re not hiding your wealth at all, more expensive resources. Redstone Automatic Farm

Good, now you have finished a hidden redstone door. Pat yourself on the back. Redstone isn't limited to only those types of uses though, as we have gone over before. We could make an automatic farm, for example. If you need something to harvest crops faster,

Automatic Farm

Start with an 8x8 square of dirt, 1 block above the ground.

Next, place a hopper that connects to a chest, with a current from a water source, made like how I made it in the picture. This will push the harvested crops to the hopper.

Place a second section of dirt, this one being 7x8. Place water inside of the cobblestone to the side, just next to the dirt.

Make the desired number of layers of farmland. It will likely look something like this.

At the very back, place a foundation for which you will make the redstone machinery.

Place the redstone down, with water in the back, and pistons just above them and further to the front. Have a redstone trail that connects them to the front for the lever to activate them.
